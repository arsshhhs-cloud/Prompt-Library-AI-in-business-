**Intended Workflow:**
Automating personalised post-consultation follow-up communications sent to patients after their appointment, replacing the manual process of nurses or reception staff writing and sending individual follow-up messages or relying on patients to remember verbal instructions.

**Problem Being Solved:** 
Research consistently shows that patients retain less than 50% of verbal information provided during a medical consultation, leading to poor adherence to follow-up instructions, missed medications, and avoidable readmissions. Clinical staff currently have limited time to write personalised follow-up summaries for every patient, meaning many patients leave without adequate written reinforcement of their care plan.

**Automation Potential:**
Very High. Upon completion of a consultation and finalisation of clinical notes in the practice management system, key outcome data can be automatically passed into this prompt to generate and dispatch a follow-up message to the patient — creating a seamless, automated post-care communication loop that requires no additional staff time and significantly improves patient health literacy and satisfaction scores.

**Risks and Limitations:**
* Clinical summaries generated by AI must accurately reflect the treating ophthalmologist's documented notes — any discrepancy between the AI-generated summary and actual clinical advice could create patient confusion or medico-legal liability for the hospital.
* Warning sign lists must be clinically validated for each condition type; a generic AI-generated list may omit condition-specific red flags (e.g. sudden vision loss post-cataract surgery) that require immediate escalation.
* SMS format limitations (160 characters) significantly constrain clinical detail — the prompt must be carefully tested to ensure critical safety information is not truncated or lost in the character limit version.

**Output**

Subject: Follow-Up and Next Steps from Your Recent Eye Consultation

Dear Mrs. Harper,

Thank you for attending your recent eye consultation. During your initial screening, we noted that your intraocular pressure was slightly elevated. While this does not confirm a diagnosis of glaucoma at this time, you are considered a "glaucoma suspect." This means it is important to monitor your eye health regularly to protect your optic nerve.

To support this, please take the following steps:

1. Collect your prescription for Xalatan eye drops from the hospital pharmacy.
2. Book a visual field test appointment for next month to assess your peripheral vision.
3. Begin using one drop of Xalatan in each eye every night as prescribed.

Please be vigilant for any warning signs such as sudden severe eye pain, blurred vision with halos around lights, or nausea. If you experience any of these symptoms, seek medical attention promptly.

If you have any questions or need assistance with your appointments, feel free to contact our office.

Best regards,  
[Your Doctor’s Name]
